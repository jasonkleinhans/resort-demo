Lake Oanob Hospitality

Where Nature, Comfort, and Adventure Meet

Nestled in the heart of Namibia's breathtaking Hardap Region, just 100 kilometres south of Windhoek, Lake Oanob Resort offers the perfect escape where comfort, adventure, and nature come together.

Surrounded by rolling hills, rugged mountains, and the tranquil waters of Lake Oanob, the resort is an ideal destination for families, couples, and travellers seeking relaxation after an unforgettable Namibian safari.

Spanning 6,200 hectares in the beautiful Hardap Region—and located just 100 kilometers south of Windhoek—Lake Oanob Resort features a premium selection of spacious luxury chalets, cozy rooms, and scenic waterfront campsites. With breathtaking lake views and genuine, heartfelt hospitality, it is the ultimate destination to relax, reset, and feel invigorated.

Whether you are celebrating a special occasion with spectacular lake views or simply looking for a peaceful retreat, our philosophy of "Body and Soul in Harmony with Nature" is reflected in every aspect of your stay. Set across 6,200 hectares of unspoiled landscape, the resort features luxurious chalets, comfortable rooms, and scenic waterfront campsites, complemented by warm Namibian hospitality.

Guests can enjoy a variety of year-round experiences, including guided game drives, hiking trails, boat cruises, canoeing, aqua-cycling, and refreshing outdoor pools, before ending the day with a sunset cocktail at the iconic Giraffe Pool Bar. At Lake Oanob Resort, every moment is designed to help you reconnect with nature, unwind in serenity, and create lasting memories.

Local Heritage & Wonders

Useful Information

Rehoboth Baster Community

Discover the unique cultural history of Rehoboth. Settled in 1870, the Baster community has a distinct cultural legal identity, ancestry, and deep heritage tied to the development of the valley landscapes surrounding the Oanob region.

Rehoboth Museum

Located in the town of Rehoboth, the museum houses archival collections documenting the historical path, natural history, and archeological findings of the Hardap territory. A recommended detour before arriving at our oasis.
